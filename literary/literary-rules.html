British Parliamentary Debate

Click any text or icon to edit or style it. Use the block parameters to hide/show text or icons and change media size or position.

Rules

Team of three.
● Topics shall be declared 10 minutes prior to each debate.
● Time limit- 10 minutes
● Languages- English
● The first round will be a general oxford pattern debate. For this round each team shall nominate two speakers to participate in the filtration process for subsequent rounds.
● A common motion shall be set for all the speakers. One speaker from each team will speak in favour of and one against the common motion.
● Speaker shall have a total time period of 3 minutes to present their arguments. No points of information shall be entertained in this round.
● Teams shall have the liberty to nominate the proposing and opposing speakers.
● The top eight teams in the cumulative score tabulations shall progress to the next round.
● The rankings shall be on the basis of average position of the speakers from the team in the group of speakers with the same motion. In case of a tie, the raw scores shall be considered and the team with a greater total shall progress.
● For the subsequent rounds, each team shall comprise of a 1st, 2nd and 3rd speaker. Each motion shall have a proposing side and an opposing side. The Proposition and Opposition sides shall be decided by a toss at the time of declaration of the topic for the debate. Speakers shall be given a time period of 4 minutes to present their individual arguments. Additionally, the 1st speaker of each side shall get a another 3 minutes to summarize the arguments of their respective team after all the speakers have spoken. A time period of 2 minutes shall be allotted at the end of each speech (except the summarizing speech) for points of information. Interruption during a speakers speech from a member of the opposing team shall not be entertained. The judge's verdict shall be final and binding.
● Vulgarity and obscenity will not be tolerated.
● All participants must reach the venue 30 minutes prior to their events.

(Oxford)
Content- 25
Argumentation- 10
Delivery- 10
Impact- 5

(BPD)

Content- 25
Argumentation- 10
Delivery- 10
Replies- 5

2. Summary (by 1st speaker)
Content- 15
Argumentation- 5
Delivery- 5
Overall Representation of stand- 5
