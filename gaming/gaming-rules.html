General Rules 

● All matches must be played in their entirety unless both teams agree to postpone or cancel the match.
● Randomized maps MUST be used. Verbal agreements will not receive staff support.
● A "Lag Test" is not required unless the non-hosting team requests one be completed. This is a test round that doesn't count and uses the standard game settings that will be used during the match. For the lag test, a map that is not scheduled for play must be used. If the game lags during this test, a new host must be chosen. If both teams cannot agree on an acceptable host, a ticket must be submitted by both teams to cancel the match.
● Teams may not delay the match for more than 5 minutes between each round/map. A round is defined as a stopping point during the match when match settings may be altered, and/or players may switch sides or change equipment.
● Hosting Rules: The higher ranked team on the ladder will host the first map. The lower ranked team will host the second map. Hosting will alternate between both teams until the final map in which the team with the most combined round wins from all prior maps will host the final map. In the event of both teams being unranked, the team with the Team ID closest to 1 will serve as the higher ranked team. In the event of combined tied rounds, the team who is closest to #1 on the ladder will host the final map. The team that is not hosting gets to choose which side to start on.
● Disconnection Clarification: The preparation phase is included in the first 30 seconds of the game.
● Exploit Clarification: Using Castle Armor Panel with Deployable Shield is a loss of that round for the Defenders.
● New Operators: The New Operators are allowed for use at this time.
● Defence Locations: In the event, a game has to be ended and restarted, a team may not choose the same Defend point on their next defence attempt if they successfully defended it. For an example, if a team successfully defends Master Bedroom on House and the game has to be restarted, that team may not choose that location on their next defence attempt. Violation will result in the loss of that round.  

DOTA 2

The DOTA 2 Tournament will be played on either a knock-out basis or a round robin basis depending on the number of participants.

Provisions
● Steam ports unlocked
● PC’s
● Extension cords

General Rules

1.    Administration and Announcements

1.1 Everybody who is authorized by SRM University to administer the competition based on its rules and regulations will be called an “admin” or “referee” in the following, the collectivity of all the admins will be called the “administration”. A list of current admins will be released soon.

1.2 Any news article posted by an admin serves as an official announcement for the competition.

2. Participants and Teams
Every real person- unless officially banned - with a valid Steam Account is allowed to Participate in the competition and will be called “participant” in the following.
Participants joining a team in order to fulfil the minimum required amount of Team members will be called a “team” in the following.

3. Commitments
3.1 By attending the competition participants acknowledge without limitation to comply with the rules and regulations, official announcement, and with the Statements and decisions made by the admins.

3.2 Every participant acknowledges the right for administration to modify the rules and regulations for adjustments at any time without notice.

3.3 Every participant has to show the needed respect towards the admin and other participants. Insults and unfair or disrespectful behaviour towards anyone, Including trash-talking, is not tolerated and will be penalized.

3.4 Every participant has to make sure that the PC has the newest version of Steam and Dota 2 installed and has to check for updates in time before each match. Unless a new version comes out right before the match, patching is not A valid reason to delay it.

3.5 Every team accepts the official schedule of the competition and declare its Ability to be available during these times.

3.6 Every team and participant has to try to win every game at every stage of the Competition. Purposefully losing for any reason is strictly forbidden.

4. Communication
4.1 All communication between participants and admins can be spoken in English language or the vernacular whichever is comfortable for both parties.

4.2 Admins can be contacted via the specified information provided by the Authorities.

Team and Players

1. Team Size
A team has to consist of five participants. Those persons will be called as “Team Members” or “Members” of the team in following.

2.  Registration
In order to sign up for the competition, a team has to fulfil all the requirements the tournament platform forces.

3.  Line Up Changes
Team members can be removed or added during the competition as long as the minimum team requirements are met all the time. There might be additional restrictions to this rule in specific competitions.

4.  In-Game Names
Each player should have a “Team Name Abbreviation” in front of their gaming/account name during all the games to be recognizable for the opponent. (Eg: OG-TopSoN, OG-Ana [belongs to the team OG]).

5.  Punctuality
5.1 If one team has not shown any sign of activity, no team member has joined the Lobby in time, all the other attempts to connect the team fail, until 15 mins after the specified match time, a default win should be requested by the other team will be taken into consideration. The default win for both games will be Confirmed by an admin under normal circumstances.

5.2 If one team is not ready to start the match (but has shown the activity) until 30 mins after the match, the opponents should report that 30 mins after the match time.

Hosting and Settings

1. Lobby Hosting
The lobby will be hosted by the admins and a digital toss will be held to decide the handicaps. The hosted lobby will be protected by a password which will be provided to the participants by the admins.

2.  Spectators
Official Broadcasters, any authorized by the admin, have the right to spectate any game without the agreement of the teams.

3.  Server Location
3.1 The default server location will be India and the second preference will be given to the region South East Asia (SEA).

3.2 The toss winning team will be able to choose a side, the second team will be able to Choose drafting order.

3.3 Each match of DOTA 2 will be a knockout game. Winner moves to the next round and the loser gets knockout (Eliminated).

 During a Game 

1.  Pauses
Each team has the right to pause the game for an acceptable duration - 10 Mins in Total for each time. (i.e. 10 mins for Radiant and 10 mins for Dire). When the number of the duration of breaks becomes too high or long, the administration may Force the continuation of the game.



2.  Disconnects and Reconnects
If a player disconnects, the game is to be paused instantly. Everyone has up to 10 mins to reconnect to the game. If the time is passed, the opposing team may continue the game, unless there is no official broadcaster inside the game.


3.  Bugs/Tools/Hacks
● Bug abuse is strictly forbidden in the game.
● The usage of any Dota-Related Tool (Ensage, Etc) aside from Steam and Dota 2 during the match is strictly forbidden.

4.  Ending
A game is finished when an Ancient is destroyed, When the majority of a team Leaves on purpose, or when the administration decides on it.(Note - A game is not Forfeited, when a player types “GG” or “FF”.) Always wait for the ancient to be Destroyed!

Game Settings

The following game will be configured according to the following lobby settings:
● Game Mode: Captain’s Mode
● Lobby Password: TBD (To be decided)

● DOTA TV Delay: 2 minutes, 0 for Admins. 

Rule Violation and Penalty

1.  Rule Violation and Penalty
Breaking of the rules or ignoring the orders of an admin may result in a penalty for a whole team or specific player. Type and amount are chosen by an admin.

2.  Possible Team / Player Punishments
● Default loss for the game
● Time penalty for the draft
● Score Reduction
● Prize Money Reduction
● Ban from the competition

Legal Process
Legal proceedings are not permitted.

PUBG Mobile

Description
The PUBG Mobile Tournament will be played on a knock-out basis.

Provisions
● Charging points
● Unblocked Wi-Fi ports 

Game Format

1. Tournament Format
The tournament will include only Squads. There will be 2 games each for every 100 people to obtain a final scorecard of every 100 people from which another 3 game round will be held. For Example: If there are 280 people registered for Solos, then there will be a total of 9 games which means 100 people will play 2 games then the remaining 100 of the 180 will play 2 games and then the remaining 80 will play 2 games and the final scorecard will be obtained of the 3 rounds of 2 games held. To give out a final scorecard Top 100 will be selected and will go in a 3 game tournament to obtain a final scorecard. Same will be followed in Squads format.

2. Team and Player Names
Team or player names that are too similar to that of another team or player are subject to be changed by the admins.
Only registered team members are allowed to play for a team or solo. Players and teams must compete under their officially registered PUBG team names and Nicknames during all official matches.

3. Game version
All players must install the newest version of the game in order to participate in the tournament. Updates must be installed before the tournament starts, so delays will be at a minimum.

4. Technical Issues
Teams are responsible for their own technical issues (hardware/internet). Matches will not be rescheduled because of technical issues and matches will be played nevertheless. In case of massive problems influencing a significant amount of players a replay of the map may be called.

5. Disqualification
To keep tournaments as efficient as possible, SRM Gaming Club reserves the right to disqualify teams. This will only be done in cases where a team purposefully stalls or attempts to block the tournament from starting.
Usage of any 3rd party programs that enhance, add, modify, or remove game appearance, colour, or files, is strictly prohibited during this event. NO EMULATORS ARE ALLOWED.
Intentionally changing your character's angle to look through a texture or object is prohibited. The intentional use of any bugs, glitches, or errors in the game is strictly forbidden and will be penalized. Any team found to be using any known exploit will forfeit their game upon the first occurrence of the exploit. If the team is found to use another known exploit for a second time and it is determined to have been done on purpose they will be removed from the event and barred from any future events.

6. Player Eligibility
All players participating in PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (henceforth PUBG) competitions must enter their PUBG game ID. The game account is needed to verify players in the room and to check if the correct player is playing. This account is the one visible inside the game when playing.
Sign-ups will be closed one hour (1h) before the tournament starts. If you or your team has not signed up before this time you will be unable to participate.

4. Details

1. Game preparations
Please resolve any problems that might occur before a match starts. Connection or hardware problems during a match could lead to a disqualification by admins. In team games, all players must be registered for their corresponding team's roster.

2. Substitute Players
No substitute players are allowed.

3. No show
If a participant cannot play at the scheduled start time, the team will have to go in handicapped.

4. Number of players
Matches should be played with 4 players, if a team is missing a player they will play the particular game handicapped.

5. Disconnects
In the case that a player(s) disconnects during a match, the team is allowed to continue to play the match at a disadvantage.

6. Forfeit
Participants can choose to forfeit a match if they wish. Forfeiting will result in 0 points from that match and possible penalty points.

7. Tiebreaker
In case of a tie in the results the first tiebreaker will be average placement of the teams during the latest round. In case they still tie the second tiebreaker will be amount of kills. If still tied the winner will be chosen randomly.

8. Restart
A re-host can only be played with admin initiation. Disconnects and lag issues will only qualify for a re-host if the issue occurs within the first 60 seconds of the game and affects a significant amount of players.

9. Results
All results will be input by the admins. If anything is unclear, participants should have screenshots available to verify the result and must inform the participant of the same.

5. Communication & Support

1. Admins
All participants must adhere to the decisions and rules of the tournament organizers, admins, and referees. All decisions are final, except in cases where the option to appeal is clearly stated. Conversations, either verbal or written, between organizers, admins or referees, and participants are confidential. No vulgarity will be entertained and may lead to disqualification.

2. Backup
In case of a failure of Internet from our side, participants are requested to charge their phones with a battery pack to avoid any issues.

3. Charger
Participants are requested to carry their own chargers, power banks and are requested to charge their phones before the tournament.

4. Issues
Before taking any decision about any issue participants are requested to inform their nearest admin and inform them of the same.
