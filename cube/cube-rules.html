2x2x2

This event deals with solving a scrambled 2x2x2 Rubik's Cube within the least time. When the group number allotted earlier to a specific group is announced, the participants of that group will be required to come to the waiting area. They'll be given a scrambled puzzle which should be solved in the least time possible, and this is repeated for 5 times. Similarly,all groups will complete their set of solves and then move on to the next event.

Judging Criteria 

After the participant stops the timer, the judge will check whether the cube is completely solved or not, without touching it. The judge will then note down the timing as is displayed on the timer. If any issues occurs in the process of checking, the OC members will be intimated.

Rules

-The participants cannot see the cube before seeking permission from the judge.
-The participants can't twist the pieces of the cube during their solve.
-The participant cannot touch the cube after the timer has been stopped.
-Signatures of both, the judge and the participant is compulsory on the score card. 
-The participants should report at the venue 60 minutes prior to the event.

Guilford Challenge

2, 3, 4, 5 and a pyraminx cubes should be solved by the team in the least time possible. Time limit for this event is 10 mins. It is a knockout event (With a Maximum of 8 teams). The timer should be started by one person in a team and stopped by the person who finishes his/her cube the last (can be the same person). For cube and timer penalties the WCA rules apply. The timer should be stopped only after completing all the puzzles. If not, the team will be disqualified.

Judging Criteria

Team blindfold

One person in the team should wear a blindfold and the other person should guide him/her to solve the puzzle by orally dictating instructions. The person who is wearing the blindfold is not allowed to see the cube until he stops the timer. Participants are expected to bring their own blindfolds as they will not be provided. The cube should be uncovered only after the timer has started.

Re-scramble the Rubik’s cube

This event deals with scrambling a solved 3x3x3 Rubik's Cube within the least time. Each person needs to have 2 Rubik’s cubes. One cube should be solved and the other cube should be submitted for scrambling. The scrambled cube should be uncovered only after starting the timer. The solved Rubik’s cube should be brought in the same state as the unscrambled cube. When the group number allotted earlier to a specific group is announced, the participants of that group will be required to come to the waiting area. They'll be given a scrambled puzzle and a solved puzzle which they have to scramble as same as given scrambled cube, and this is repeated for 5 times. Similarly,all groups will complete their set of solves and then move on to the next event.  

Rules

-The participants cannot see the cube before seeking permission from the judge.
-The participants can't twist the pieces of the cube during their solve.
-The participant cannot touch the cube after the timer has been stopped.
-Signatures of both, the judge and the participant is compulsory on the score card.
-The cube and timer penalties apply as per WCA rules.  
-The participants should report at the venue 60 minutes prior to the event.

Judging Criteria

After the participant stops the timer, the judge will check whether the cube is completely solved or not, without touching it. The judge will then note down the timing as is displayed on the timer. If any issues occurs in the process of checking, the OC members will be intimated.

Participant Rules:

1. The competitor limit is 120. 

2. To participate in any event the participant should get a Milan Pass. 

    • To get the Milan pass the participant should bring their ID proof     (school ID/ driving license/ Aadhar Card/ College ID etc.) 

    • Milan passes may also be purchased online. A link for the same     will be available on the Milan website. 

3. The participant should get their own puzzles. 

4.The participants should report at the venue 60 minutes prior to the event.

5. This competition does not come under WCA and hence it is an unofficial competition. The results of this competition will not be added to the WCA profiles of the participants.

Event-wise Rules:

1. Common Rules for all the events (1-10) : https://www.worldcubeassociation.org/regulations/

2. The decision of the Organizing committee is final and any ambiguity will not be entertained. It might also lead to disqualification of the participant.
